Insurance Claims · Storm Damage

Storm damage?
Get inspected first.

Most homeowners call their insurance company before they know what's actually wrong with the roof. That's how claims get denied — or settled for half what they're worth. Here's the right order of operations, from a contractor who runs this process every week.

Book a Free Inspection
Licensed · MHIC #115973 Bonded Insured Free Inspection
1

Get a roof inspection first.

Before you ever pick up the phone to your insurer. A licensed contractor walks every plane of the roof and tells you honestly whether you have damage that meets a claim threshold. No damage = no claim filed = no premium hike.

2

Document everything, fast.

Exterior shots, ceiling stains, fallen branches, hail-strike marks on gutters and siding. Date-stamped. The first 24 hours after damage is when documentation is cleanest and most credible to an adjuster.

3

Don't sign with the first knock on your door.

Real local contractors don't need to door-chase after a storm. If someone shows up offering to "handle everything" before you've even called your insurer, walk away. Out-of-state storm-chasers disappear once the work gets hard.

4

Have your contractor meet the adjuster.

Adjusters miss damage every single day. Having an experienced roofer on the ladder with them is the biggest single factor in whether your claim pays out fairly — or under-pays you by thousands.

5

Let your contractor handle the rest.

Once the claim is approved, we manage code upgrades, permits, dumpsters, and final inspection. You shouldn't be project-managing your own roof replacement on top of a stressful claim.

FAQ

Plain answers.

A few of the questions we get most. Don't see yours? Call or text the office and we'll get back same-day.

How long does a roof replacement take?+
Most single-family re-roofs are a one-day job — tear-off in the morning, underlayment by midday, shingles and ridge by evening. Larger or steeper roofs run two days. We schedule around weather and keep your driveway and landscaping protected the whole time.
Do you handle the insurance claim?+
Yes. We document the damage, file the supporting estimate, meet the adjuster on the roof when needed, and follow the claim through approval. You sign the work agreement — we do the paperwork.
What areas of Maryland do you serve?+
Primarily Anne Arundel County — Annapolis, Severna Park, Glen Burnie, Pasadena, Crofton, Crownsville, Edgewater, and Arnold — plus surrounding parts of Howard, Prince George's, and Queen Anne's. If you're a little outside that radius, call us — we'll be straight with you.
Are estimates really free?+
Free, on-site, and written — not a phone-call ballpark. We walk the roof, measure, photograph what we find, and email a line-item proposal within 48 hours. No deposit, no obligation.
